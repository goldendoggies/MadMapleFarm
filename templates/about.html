About our Team

Please contact us for any interest in joining our community of equestrians or if you have any questions about sales, leases, or simply want to talk shop! We are pleased to be a part of the broader horse world and interested to work with our client and students to expand and grow the sport.

August C. Torsilieri
Co-Founder and Head Trainer
Head of Operations, Sales and Services

August ‘Gus’ Torsilieri began riding at his family farm at a young age. Growing up to a non “horsey” family he began his education with the US Pony club. After attaining his A rating he began a tutelage with Jean Paul Murat.

After opening his own stables at 18, August focused on retraining problem horses and breaking in young stock. Over the years August grew his business, Torsilieri Show Stables, to a 30+ horse training business in Pittstown NJ. Throughout his career he has learned and been mentored by top professionals such as Devin Ryan, Nona Garson, Anne Kursinski and Stephan Cheret.

August’s personal riding career has taken him to the FEI 3* levels on Samy du Reverdy, breaking into the top 100 on the Rolex Ranking list, winning numerous grand prix classes and open ranking classes. Young horse production has always been a passion of August’s and has produced many young horses to the national finals and developed and sold them on to great results. In addition, his students have been seen in the high amateur classes, U25 classes and junior jumper ranks.

When not riding and training, August enjoys cooking, golf and mowing his yard.

Alexandra K. Hird
Co-Founder and Senior Trainer
Equestrian Director

Alex started loving horses when she was 7. She began at a riding school near her childhood home in Phoenixville PA. She and her best friend grew up riding around the golf course across the street and running through trails and corn fields.

Alex began her formal training as a rider with Dawn Corrado at Valley Hill Farm in Chester Springs PA. She found her very first horse, a 5 year old Thoroughbred named Kodak Moment. That was the beginning of Alex’s love and respect of the thoroughbred breed.

During and after College, Alex worked for and rode for Frank, Mary, and Laura Chapot in Neshanic Station, NJ. It was this fortunate opportunity that took Alex to all the major shows along the east coast. It was also here that Alex developed an appreciation for young horses and training.

12 years later, Alex decided to start her own business. Veritas Farms had a myriad of students and horses, some of whom are still with Alex to this day. She has since jumped many Grand Prixs and just ventured into the International Derbies.
